## Summary

Welcome to **Threat Intel** — an open, continuously updated feed of active cybersecurity threats sourced from the most authoritative public databases. This site tracks CVEs, zero-days, ransomware campaigns, and exploitation activity so defenders can act fast.

The goal is simple: cut through the noise and surface what matters. Every post includes severity context, affected systems, impact assessment, and actionable mitigation steps — because a threat advisory without a fix is just alarm fatigue.

## What We Track

- **CVEs with CVSS >= 7.0** — High and critical vulnerabilities with known or likely exploitation paths
- **CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV)** — Vulnerabilities confirmed exploited in the wild
- **Zero-days** — Vulnerabilities disclosed before a vendor patch exists
- **Active ransomware campaigns** — TTPs, IOCs, and affected sectors
- **Supply chain threats** — Compromised packages, build pipelines, and upstream dependencies

## Sources

All intelligence is drawn from public, authoritative sources:

- [NVD — National Vulnerability Database](https://nvd.nist.gov/)

## Severity Rating

Posts are color-coded by severity:

| Badge | Level | Typical CVSS | Meaning |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|
| CRITICAL | Critical | 9.0–10.0 | Remote code execution, no auth required, or actively exploited |
| HIGH | High | 7.0–8.9 | Significant impact, likely exploitation path exists |
| MEDIUM | Medium | 4.0–6.9 | Real risk, but requires conditions or limited exposure |
| LOW | Low | 0.1–3.9 | Minor risk, informational, or mitigated by default configs |
